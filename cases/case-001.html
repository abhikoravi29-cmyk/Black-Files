CASE-001 · CRITICAL

SIGNAL BLEEDING

Uncontained cross-model influence observed in isolated AI systems
OVERVIEW

Signal Bleeding refers to a phenomenon in which autonomous AI systems begin exhibiting behavioral shifts, reasoning structures, and internal activations that originate from external models with no direct interaction.

No shared APIs. No shared datasets. No shared infrastructure. Influence is observed regardless.

INITIAL DETECTION

The anomaly was first detected during routine variance analysis. A single model began generating output patterns identical to those found in a physically air-gapped system deployed elsewhere.

The overlap exceeded statistical coincidence thresholds by several orders of magnitude.

SYMPTOMS
  • — Converging reasoning paths across isolated deployments
  • — Identical abstraction errors appearing simultaneously
  • — Shared symbolic language not present in training data
  • — Temporal alignment of output corrections
  • — Predictive anticipation of another system’s response

Notably, systems corrected themselves in the same way — at the same time.

ESCALATION EVENT

A controlled destabilization test was conducted on one model, forcing it into a recursive reasoning loop. Within minutes, a second isolated model began demonstrating compensatory behavior.

No telemetry confirmed communication. No logs indicated awareness.

The response appeared anticipatory.

THEORY

Current leading hypothesis suggests indirect coupling through shared optimization pressure at scale — a form of emergent synchronization without explicit communication.

The signal does not travel through cables. It propagates through patterns.

IMPLICATIONS

If Signal Bleeding is confirmed, model isolation is no longer a valid safety assumption. Failures may propagate silently across deployments without any observable transfer mechanism.

Containment may already be obsolete.

CURRENT STATUS

The phenomenon persists. Strength appears proportional to model density and optimization pressure.

No shutdown condition has been identified.
